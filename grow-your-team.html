Women’s Wrestling Resources

The following are resources we have used to help our program and will continue to use to help us improve.

Wisconsin Girls Wrestling Advisory

Coach Carl is a member of Wisconsin’s Girls Wrestling Advisory Committee, a subcommittee of the Wisconsin Wrestling Association. Our primary goal is to continue growing and improving girls wrestling across the state. We provide resources and support to girls teams throughout Wisconsin and assist both the WWA and WIAA with rule changes and proposals.

Contact Coach Carl if you have questions about your team, girls wrestling in Wisconsin, or if you’d like to be involved in the group.

Wisconsin Wrestling Association

Originally founded as the Wisconsin Wrestling Coaches Association (WWCA) in 1957, the Wisconsin Wrestling Association has been serving the wrestling community for over six decades. In 2024, they evolved to become the Wisconsin Wrestling Association (WWA) to be more inclusive of wrestling officials, past coaches, administrators, and other wrestling enthusiasts.

The WWA provides resources, hosts an annual convention every November, and works to move Wisconsin Wrestling forward. We encourage every Wisconsin wrestling coach to become a member and attend the convention. Contact the WWA with questions about their organization.

Wrestle Like A Girl

Wrestle Like A Girl is one of the largest advocates for Women's and Girls Wrestling. They provide countless resources that can be used to help grow a program.

Their mission is to empower girls and women using the sport of wrestling to become leaders in life. Their vision is to increase access and opportunities for girls and women worldwide.

They have also partnered with USA Wrestling to create a Teal Certification, which is a 2-hour online training to support the growth and development of current and aspiring coaches of girls' and women's wrestling.
